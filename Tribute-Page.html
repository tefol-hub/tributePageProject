Dr. Milton Friedman

A Bastion of Free Market economics

Here's are some of the highlights in Dr Friedman's life

  • 1912 - Born in Brooklyn, New York
  • 1932 - Graduates from Rutgers University with a Bachelor's degree in economics
  • 1935 - Receives a Master's degree in economics from the University of Chicago
  • 1937 - Earns a Ph.D. in economics from Columbia University
  • 1940 - Begins teaching economics at the University of Wisconsin-Madison
  • 1943 - Joins the Treasury Department's Division of Tax Research
  • 1945 - Publishes his influential essay "The Role of Government in Education"
  • 1951 - Publishes his book "Essays in Positive Economics"
  • 1955 - Becomes a professor of economics at the University of Chicago
  • 1962 - Publishes his book "Capitalism and Freedom"
  • 1969 - Begins serving as an advisor to President Richard Nixon
  • 1976 - Receives the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences for his contributions to the field of consumption analysis
  • 1980 - Publishes his book "Free to Choose" with his wife Rose Friedman
  • 1991 - Retires from teaching at the University of Chicago
  • 2006 - Passes away at the age of 94

Milton Friedman was a renowned economist known for his advocacy of free-market principles and his contributions to economic theory. His work emphasized the importance of individual freedom, limited government intervention, and the power of voluntary exchanges in driving economic prosperity. Through his books, articles, and lectures, Friedman had a significant influence on economic policy and public discourse.

"A society that puts equality before freedom wil get neither. A society that puts freedom before equality will get a high degree of both. --A famous quote by Dr. Friedman"
